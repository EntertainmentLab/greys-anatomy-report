ATE vs AME Estimates Comparison

Analysis Purpose:
This analysis compares treatment effect estimates from two approaches: 1) Average Treatment Effects (ATE) from linear models (emmeans contrasts), and 2) Average Marginal Effects (AME) estimates that incorporate interaction with heat index. ATE estimates show standard treatment effects assuming no moderation by heat exposure. AME estimates show treatment effects averaged across all heat index values, accounting for potential moderation. Both sets of estimates are FDR-corrected within each contrast type and method separately.
