ATE vs AME Estimates Comparison

Analysis Purpose:
This analysis compares treatment effect estimates from two approaches: 1) Average Treatment Effects (ATE) from linear models (emmeans contrasts), and 2) Average Marginal Effects (AME) estimates that incorporate interaction with heat index. ATE estimates show standard treatment effects assuming no moderation by heat exposure. AME estimates show treatment effects averaged across all heat index values, accounting for potential moderation. Both sets of estimates are FDR-corrected within each contrast type and method separately.

Multiplatform vs. Treatment Only: Show Recommendation Comparison

Analysis Purpose:
This analysis examines how viewing social media content about a show (Handoff condition) influences people's willingness to recommend the show compared to just viewing the episode content (Treatment condition). We compare two approaches: (1) a regular linear model and (2) an Average Marginal Effects (AME) model that accounts for potential moderation by heat index exposure. The Handoff coefficient represents the effect of seeing social media content about the show, relative to the Treatment reference group.
